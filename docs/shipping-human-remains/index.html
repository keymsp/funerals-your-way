Shipping human remains or cremated remains often involves time-sensitive coordination between several parties, including funeral homes, airlines, medical offices, and consulates when international transport is involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Shipping Human Remains

How long does it take to ship human remains?

Timeframes vary depending on location and documentation, but most arrangements are completed within several days once all requirements are in place.

What documents are required?

Typical requirements include a death certificate, transit permit, and in some cases consulate authorization. We handle all documentation and coordination.

Is embalming required?

Embalming is generally required for international shipping and some airline transport. We will explain what is needed based on your situation.

Can cremated remains be shipped or carried on a plane?

Yes. Cremated remains can be shipped or carried, but must meet airline and TSA requirements. We provide guidance to ensure compliance.

Do I need a receiving funeral home?

A receiving funeral home is typically required when shipping human remains to another state or country. For cremated remains (ashes), a receiving funeral home is not always necessary, depending on the destination and method of transport. We will explain what is required and coordinate everything based on your situation.
