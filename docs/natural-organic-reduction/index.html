Human Composting in California

Natural Organic Reduction, Legal Status & What Families Should Know

Natural Organic Reduction (often referred to as human composting) is an emerging end-of-life option that returns the body to the earth through a carefully managed, natural process. By combining organic materials with controlled microbial activity, the body is gently transformed into nutrient-rich soil—without flame, chemicals, or permanent burial structures.

While Natural Organic Reduction has been approved in California, it is not yet operational locally. Many families currently coordinate services with licensed facilities in other states.

How Natural Organic Reduction Works

Natural Organic Reduction follows a carefully controlled, step-by-step process that transforms the body into nutrient-rich soil over time. Each phase is managed by trained professionals to ensure safety, environmental responsibility, and dignity.

Placement in a Vessel

The body is placed in a closed, reusable vessel with organic materials such as wood chips and plant matter. These materials create the structure and balance needed to support natural microbial activity.

Naturally Transformed

Over several weeks, aerobic decomposition occurs—similar to natural processes found in forest ecosystems. Temperature, airflow, and moisture are carefully controlled to ensure proper conditions and safety.

Resting and Stabilization

After the active phase, the material is allowed to rest and stabilize. This results in clean, nutrient-rich soil that meets established safety standards.

Returning the Soil

Once complete, the soil may be returned to the family, depending on state regulations. Many families use it for memorial plantings or conservation efforts.

Natural Organic Reduction offers a structured, environmentally focused alternative to traditional burial and cremation. While this option is not yet available locally, many families choose cremation services or burial at sea for their simplicity and immediate availability.

Environmental Considerations and Benefits

Many families explore Natural Organic Reduction because of its reduced environmental impact compared to traditional burial or cremation.

Traditional cremation requires sustained high heat and fossil fuel use. Conventional burial often involves embalming chemicals, hardwood caskets, and concrete vaults that remain in the ground long term. Natural Organic Reduction avoids flame-based processes and does not require permanent burial containers.

The controlled transformation process significantly reduces carbon emissions compared to flame cremation, and a portion of the carbon is retained within the resulting soil. For families who value sustainability, this option offers a way to align end-of-life decisions with environmental principles.

Beyond environmental impact, some families find meaning in the idea of contributing to land restoration or memorial plantings. The process allows for a tangible, earth-centered legacy.

Frequently Asked Questions About Natural Organic Reduction

Families considering Natural Organic Reduction often have practical and legal questions, particularly while the process is not yet available in California. The following answers address common concerns about availability, timing, safety, and planning.

Is Natural Organic Reduction legal in California?

Natural Organic Reduction has been approved in California and is expected to become available beginning in 2027, pending final regulatory implementation. Licensed facilities are not yet operating within the state.

Where is Natural Organic Reduction currently available?

Natural Organic Reduction is currently offered in select states, including Washington. Families in California may coordinate services with licensed facilities in those states.

How long does the process take?

The active transformation phase typically takes several weeks, followed by a resting and stabilization period. The full process generally takes about 8–12 weeks, depending on facility protocols.

Is the soil safe?

Yes. Licensed facilities monitor temperature, airflow, and microbial activity to ensure pathogen reduction and compliance with established safety standards before soil is released.

Can families receive the soil?

Depending on state regulations and facility policies, families may receive the resulting soil for memorial plantings or conservation use.

How is Natural Organic Reduction different from green burial?

Green burial places the body directly into the earth without embalming or vaults. Natural Organic Reduction transforms the body into soil within a controlled environment before it is returned to the earth.

Can I pre-plan Natural Organic Reduction?

Yes. Advance planning allows families to document their wishes and coordinate transportation arrangements while the process remains unavailable within California.
