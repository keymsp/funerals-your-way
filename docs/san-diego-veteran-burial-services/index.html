San Diego Veteran Burial Services

Funerals Your Way provides veteran burial services in San Diego, including coordination for military honors and national cemetery interment when eligible. Veteran burial follows standard burial practices, with additional considerations for honors, eligibility, and cemetery coordination.

Families may choose burial at a national cemetery such as Miramar, or at a public or private cemetery of their choice. Our licensed funeral directors assist with each step, ensuring arrangements are handled with clarity, respect, and attention to service.

Veteran Burial Benefits & National Cemeteries

Eligible veterans may qualify for burial benefits through national and state programs. These benefits can include interment at a national cemetery, military honors, and a government-provided headstone or grave marker.

The United States maintains more than 130 national cemeteries. In San Diego, families may be eligible for burial at Miramar National Cemetery, depending on service history, discharge status, and availability.

Our funeral directors assist families in understanding eligibility and coordinate directly with the appropriate agencies and cemeteries to ensure arrangements are completed correctly and with respect.

Veteran Burial Options & Pricing

We offer three common veteran burial options. The appropriate choice depends on whether a service is desired, the timing of burial, and family preferences. All options are handled by licensed funeral directors and may be arranged at a national, public, or private cemetery.

  • Direct Burial (Unattended Interment) — A simple, dignified burial without a service or family present. All arrangements and cemetery coordination are handled on your behalf.
  • Graveside Service with Honors — A brief graveside service with family present. Military honors may be included, providing a meaningful and respectful farewell at the cemetery.
  • Traditional Burial with Service — A full-service burial including viewing, ceremony, and procession prior to interment. Allows time for gathering, remembrance, and a more traditional farewell.

Veteran Burial Service Customizations

Every veteran burial service can be personalized to reflect individual service, beliefs, and family wishes. While the burial options above outline the core arrangements, families may choose to add or adjust elements to create a more personal tribute.

Available customizations may include:

  • Calling hours or visitation
  • Printed programs
  • Additional clergy or officiant
  • Military or patriotic music
  • Live musician or vocalist
  • Recorded music selections
  • Floral arrangements
  • Obituary and death notice assistance
  • Prayer cards or memorial keepsakes
  • Horse-drawn carriage
  • Coordination with honor guard or ceremonial elements

Our funeral directors work closely with families to ensure any requested additions are handled respectfully and in accordance with cemetery and military guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions About Veteran Burial Services

Do veterans qualify for burial at a national cemetery?

Eligible veterans may qualify for burial at a national cemetery depending on service history and discharge status. Eligibility is determined by the Department of Veterans Affairs and requires verification.

Is burial at a national cemetery free for veterans?

National cemetery burial benefits may include the gravesite, opening and closing of the grave, a government-provided headstone or marker, and perpetual care at no cost to the family.

Can military honors be included with a veteran burial?

Military honors may be provided for eligible veterans and typically include the folding and presentation of the American flag and the playing of Taps. Honors are coordinated separately from the funeral service.

Can families choose burial outside a national cemetery?

Yes. Veteran burial services may be arranged at national, public, or private cemeteries based on family preference and eligibility.

What documents are required for veteran burial benefits?

Military discharge documentation, such as a DD-214, is typically required to verify eligibility for veteran burial benefits and honors.
