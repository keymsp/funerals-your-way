What to Do When a Death Occurs in San Diego

Contact us any time – we have a funeral director on call 24 hours a day. We are always available, day or night. Our licensed funeral directors can answer any questions you may have, offer guidance, and help explain what your next steps are.

  • Hospice can assist until we arrive if someone dies at home. When someone dies at home, hospice or a doctor can provide initial support. When you contact us, we will prepare to take the next steps, including a dignified transfer when the time is right.
  • If someone dies at a hospital or care facility, staff will assist you with next steps. When someone you love dies at a hospital, skilled nursing facility, or assisted living facility, the staff will take care of notifying the right people. Then, we work with the facility to arrange everything needed.
  • We can start arrangements over the phone or online. Many families wish to start the service arrangements without having to meet in person right away. This gives you the opportunity to work at your own pace as a family and focus on the most important things.
  • No immediate decisions are required. There’s no need to rush into making a decision. We assist families by helping you take things one step at a time. We help explain the options available to you so you can make decisions when you are ready.

Clear, Transparent Pricing

We understand how worrying unexpected costs can be. At Funerals Your Way, we provide families with pricing and policies before making any commitments.

Though every case is different, there are general trends. Typically, cremation is the most cost-effective option and burials can rack up costs with cemetery, merchandise and service fees. We explain pricing and inclusions, go over all the options, and provide our General Price List to families so they can evaluate their choices.

We will not pressure you to make any selections or commitments and there are no hidden costs. You can take the time you need to make the right decisions, and we urge you to keep control of the process.

Families can meet with a licensed director and discuss all the prices and options as many as they want without any pressure.

We believe families deserve clear explanations and straightforward pricing before any decisions are made.

For families who want more detail, you can review our Cremation Pricing, Burial Pricing, or view our General Price List, which outlines all available services and options. A licensed funeral director is always available to review pricing and answer questions with no obligation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to go to the funeral home in person?

No. Many San Diego families prefer to make arrangements over the phone or the internet. This allows families to set their own pace and eliminates the need to worry about traffic or complicated schedules. If you would like to meet in person, we can set that up, but it isn’t necessary.

How soon can you have someone picked up in San Diego?

Typically, we can schedule transportation within a couple hours of receiving your notification and completing the necessary legal paperwork. However, it depends on the circumstances and the location. One of our licensed funeral directors is on call 24/7 and can help you with the next steps.

How long does it take to get someone cremated in San Diego?

There are a number of factors involved in this answer, such as permits, scheduling, and the kind of service you choose. Usually, we are able to perform the actual cremation service within a few days of receiving all the required paperwork. We identify expected timelines in advance so families have a clear understanding of what to expect. That being said most families have the ash back within 1.5 weeks.

Are cremations less expensive than burials?

Generally, cremations are less expensive than burials because there are no additional costs involved with burial merchandise, cemetery plots, or burial vaults. However, we explain the services involved with clear pricing before decisions are made, as costs will vary depending on the selected options.

Can we schedule memorial services for later?

Absolutely. The flexibility cremation provides is one of the reasons many families in San Diego opt for it. Memorial services or celebration of life services can be held in days, weeks, or even months.

Do you provide services accommodating different cultures or religions?

Absolutely, we do this all the time. There are many diverse cultures in San Diego, and we assist families of many cultures, religions, and personal preferences. We provide services in collaboration with families and community representatives in a manner of respect to customs and traditions.

Do you partner with San Diego hospitals and hospice services?

Yes. We frequently coordinate with county hospitals, nursing homes, assisted living facilities, and hospice services. We take care of the communication and logistics so families do not have to worry about it.

What if relatives are out of town?

Some of the families we work with have relatives outside of San Diego or even out of state. We have streamlined the process of remote coordination and keep everyone in the family updated to ensure the continuity of the process.

Is aftercare support included?

Yes. We offer aftercare support to assist families with the numerous obligations that follow a loss such as notifications and documentation. This support is available no matter what type of service is selected.
