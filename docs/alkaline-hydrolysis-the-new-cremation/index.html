Alkaline Hydrolysis (Water Cremation) in California: What Families Should Know

In recent years, alkaline hydrolysis — sometimes called “water cremation” or “aquamation” — has emerged as an alternative method of final disposition. Families exploring environmentally conscious options may come across this process and wonder how it compares to traditional cremation or burial. Those reviewing their options may also wish to compare it with traditional cremation services available in San Diego.

In California, alkaline hydrolysis is legally permitted, though availability remains limited. Understanding how the process works, how it differs from flame-based cremation, and what options are currently accessible in San Diego can help families make informed and thoughtful decisions.

What Is Alkaline Hydrolysis?

Alkaline hydrolysis, sometimes referred to as water cremation or aquamation, is a method of final disposition that uses water, heat, and alkaline compounds to accelerate the natural decomposition process. Unlike traditional flame-based cremation, which relies on high heat and combustion, alkaline hydrolysis uses a controlled water-based process within a sealed stainless-steel chamber.

At the completion of the process, the remaining bone fragments are dried and processed into a form similar in appearance to traditional cremated remains. Families may choose to keep the remains in an urn, place them in a cemetery or columbarium, or scatter them in accordance with local and state regulations.

While the scientific process differs from flame cremation, the final options for memorialization are generally similar.

Is Water Cremation Legal in California?

Alkaline hydrolysis is legally permitted in California. However, availability varies by region, and not all funeral providers or facilities offer this option. Because the equipment required for the process is specialized, it is typically available only in select locations.

Families in San Diego who are considering alkaline hydrolysis may wish to inquire about current availability, transportation requirements, and associated timelines. As with traditional cremation, proper authorization and documentation are required before the process can proceed.

While water cremation is legally recognized in California, traditional cremation and burial remain the most widely accessible options throughout San Diego County.

How Alkaline Hydrolysis Differs from Traditional Cremation

The primary difference between alkaline hydrolysis and traditional cremation lies in the method used to reduce the body to remains. Traditional cremation uses high heat and flame, while alkaline hydrolysis uses a water-based process combined with heat and alkaline compounds within a sealed chamber.

From a family’s perspective, however, the final result is similar. In both processes, the remaining bone fragments are returned to the family in a form suitable for placement in an urn, burial, or scattering according to local regulations.

Ultimately, the choice often comes down to availability, personal beliefs, and practical considerations rather than significant differences in memorial options.

Environmental Considerations

Some families researching alkaline hydrolysis are motivated by environmental concerns. Because the process does not rely on flame combustion, it does not produce the same type of emissions associated with traditional cremation. This has led to discussions about potential environmental benefits.

At the same time, modern cremation practices are regulated and designed to meet strict environmental standards. Advances in filtration systems and emissions controls have significantly reduced environmental impact over time.

For families in San Diego who prioritize sustainability, options such as green burial, traditional cremation, or alkaline hydrolysis may all be considered as part of a broader conversation about values and legacy. Each method has practical, legal, and logistical considerations that should be reviewed carefully. Families prioritizing sustainability may also explore our Green Burial Options in San Diego.

The most appropriate choice often depends on personal priorities, availability, and the guidance of a licensed funeral professional.

Availability of Water Cremation in San Diego County

Although alkaline hydrolysis is legally permitted in California, availability in San Diego County remains limited. The specialized equipment required for the process is not widely installed, and in some cases, transportation to another facility may be necessary.

Families considering water cremation should confirm current availability, associated timelines, and any additional coordination that may be required. Because options can change over time, speaking directly with a licensed funeral provider can help clarify what is presently accessible.

For many San Diego families, traditional cremation and burial continue to be the most readily available and established options. Exploring all choices thoughtfully allows families to make decisions that align with their values while also considering practicality and timing.

Making an Informed and Thoughtful Decision

Choosing a final disposition method is a personal decision shaped by beliefs, environmental considerations, family traditions, and practical realities. Whether a family is exploring alkaline hydrolysis, traditional cremation, or burial, clarity and understanding are essential.

While newer options like water cremation continue to gain attention, traditional cremation and burial remain the most widely available choices throughout San Diego County. Each option carries its own logistical, legal, and emotional considerations.

Families who are researching their options are encouraged to ask questions, review current availability, and consider what aligns best with their values and circumstances. Taking time to gather accurate information can bring peace of mind and prevent uncertainty during an already difficult period.

Frequently Asked Questions About Water Cremation

Families exploring alkaline hydrolysis often have practical questions about how the process works, whether it is available locally, and how memorial options compare to traditional cremation. Because this method is less familiar to many people, clear and accurate information can help reduce uncertainty and support thoughtful decision-making.

Is alkaline hydrolysis legal in California?

Yes. Alkaline hydrolysis is legally permitted in California. However, availability varies by region, and not all facilities currently offer the process.

Is water cremation the same as traditional cremation?

No. Traditional cremation uses high heat and flame, while alkaline hydrolysis uses a water-based process with heat and alkaline compounds. The final remains returned to the family are similar in form and may be memorialized in the same ways.

Can remains from alkaline hydrolysis be scattered at sea or buried?

Yes. The processed remains may be placed in an urn, buried, or scattered in accordance with California and federal regulations. However, families considering burial at sea should be aware that the texture of remains from alkaline hydrolysis can differ from traditional flame-based cremation. In some cases, the remains may have a finer consistency and can behave differently in water. Families interested in ocean memorials can learn more about our Burial at Sea Services in San Diego.

For ocean scattering, biodegradable urns or other approved containers may be recommended to ensure proper dispersion and compliance with maritime guidelines.

Is alkaline hydrolysis considered more environmentally friendly?

Some families consider it an environmentally conscious option because it does not involve combustion. However, modern cremation practices also follow environmental regulations and emission standards.
