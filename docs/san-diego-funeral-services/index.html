Frequently Asked Questions About Funeral Services

When beginning to work with funeral service providers, families typically have the same questions. Below are common questions families ask when first exploring funeral services.

What is the first phone call to a funeral home like?

The first call is designed to be a listening exercise. There will be questions to answer and a description of the next steps. You do not have to make any decisions or commit to any services during that call.

How much do funeral services cost in San Diego?

The costs vary depending on the service chosen. We offer pricing to explain the options clearly and to make sure that the family is on the same page before any decisions are made.

Can I make arrangements without having to go to a funeral home?

Yes. Many families do all their arrangements over the phone or online. If you prefer having an in-person meeting, that can be arranged, but it is not a requirement.

Do funeral services have to happen right away after a death?

No. Services can happen days or even weeks later depending on the family’s wishes, religious factors, and logistics. We help with the planning around what fits best for your family.

Can you ask questions before deciding on a funeral home?

Of course. There is no pressure to make a decision, and we think that asking questions is a vital part of the process. Our goal is to help families feel educated and at ease before making a decision.

What support is offered after the funeral services?

Families are provided aftercare support, which includes guidance on next steps, documentation, notifications, and other practical concerns after a death.

Must I make the decision whether to have a burial or a cremation right away?

No. There is no need to make that decision immediately. It is our job to outline the options and help you keep moving forward at a pace that feels best for you.
