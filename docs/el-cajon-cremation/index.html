Common Questions After a Death in El Cajon

Are meetings at the funeral home mandatory?

No. Most families in El Cajon handle everything via phone or online. This is especially nice since El Cajon can be a long drive. If you would like in-person meetings, those can be arranged, but are never mandatory.

How soon can you take our loved one into your care when they pass in El Cajon?

After we are notified and the proper approvals are secured, we do our best to plan transportation. Depending on the situation, timing can differ, but we often collaborate with El Cajon hospitals, care centers, and hospice services to prevent delays. Very often, we can get to the location in less than two hours.

What do you do when someone dies at home in El Cajon?

If the person was receiving hospice services, the hospice caregiver or doctor will assist with the first steps. If the person dies and was not receiving hospice care, then the local police have to be called to find out what the steps are. Once we have a release or an authorization, we can take your loved one into our care.

How long does it take to get a loved one cremated?

Once all the necessary documentation has been completed, cremation usually takes only a couple of days to occur. We are communicative during the entire process so you’ll know when to expect the cremated remains to be returned. Generally, the whole process takes about 1.5 weeks.

Is it cheaper to be cremated than to be buried?

Generally speaking, yes. A burial will almost always have extra expenses than a cremation, such as a casket, a burial vault, and fees for the burial plot. With cremation, it’s more straightforward and families can even skip the memorial services and do them at their convenience.

Can we do a memorial service at a later date after the cremation?

Of course. It’s common for families in El Cajon to do cremation and then schedule a memorial service/celebration of life for a later date. This is especially the case when family members need time to travel or coordinate to be available.

Do you coordinate with the churches and cemeteries in El Cajon?

Yes. We often help families with memorial services, cemetery services, and church services in El Cajon and East County. We do the scheduling and prepare the necessary paperwork to help streamline the process.
