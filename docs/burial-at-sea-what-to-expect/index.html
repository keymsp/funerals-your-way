What to Expect on the Day of a Burial at Sea

What to Expect on the Day of a Burial at Sea

Planning a burial at sea can feel unfamiliar, especially during a time of loss. Families often ask what the day will be like and how everything unfolds. Our role is to guide you through the experience with care and clarity, so you can focus on honoring your loved one without uncertainty.

If you are still exploring your options, you may find it helpful to learn more about our burial at sea services in San Diego, including the types of ceremonies and arrangements available.

Each service is thoughtfully planned and gently paced. While every family’s wishes are unique, the outline below reflects what most families experience on the day of a burial or ash scattering at sea from San Diego.

If you have questions at any point, you’re welcome to speak with a funeral director anytime at 619-550-7807.

Arrival and Boarding

Families typically arrive at the harbor shortly before departure. A funeral director or experienced attendant will greet you, confirm final details, and assist with boarding. Seating is arranged so families can remain together.

Before leaving the dock, the captain provides a brief overview of the trip and safety information. This is also a time to ask any last questions or make small adjustments to the ceremony.

The Journey Out to Sea

The vessel departs San Diego Bay and travels calmly toward the designated ocean location. Many trips pass along familiar landmarks such as Shelter Island, Harbor Island, Point Loma, and beneath the Coronado Bridge before reaching open water.

This portion of the journey often feels reflective. Families may sit quietly, share memories, or simply take in the ocean surroundings as the coastline gradually fades from view.

The Ceremony

Once the vessel reaches the appropriate offshore location, the ceremony begins. Families may choose a simple, quiet moment or a more guided service led by a funeral director.

Ceremonies may include:

  • Brief words of remembrance
  • Readings or prayers
  • Personal reflections shared aloud
  • Music played softly
  • Moments of silence

For ash scattering or placement of a biodegradable urn, the committal is handled with care and respect. Families may participate as much or as little as they feel comfortable. Fresh flowers or rose petals are often placed on the water following the committal.

There is no rushing. This moment is given the time and space it deserves.

Time for Reflection

After the ceremony, the vessel remains still for a short period. This allows families time for private reflection, final words, or quiet observation of the ocean.

Many families describe this as one of the most meaningful moments of the day — a pause where emotions settle and the significance of the farewell is fully felt.

The Return to Harbor

After this time of reflection, the vessel begins the return journey to the harbor. Many families remain quietly seated, while others continue sharing memories or simply take in the ocean.

The return often feels calmer and more settled, providing a gentle transition back after the service.

After the Service

Following the burial at sea, all required permits and documentation are handled on your behalf including the California disposition permit and required filings. Families receive confirmation that the service was conducted in accordance with state and federal regulations.

If additional arrangements, memorial planning, or aftercare support are needed, our funeral directors are always available to assist.

Memorial Certificate

After the burial or ash scattering at sea, families may request a Memorial Certificate that includes the date of the ceremony, the coordinates of the scattering location, and the name of your loved one. Many families find comfort in having this tangible reminder of the farewell shared on the water.

Photographs from the ceremony may also be provided upon request, offering a meaningful keepsake for family members who were unable to attend.

A Gentle, Personalized Experience

Every burial at sea is different. Some services are quiet and private; others include shared words, faith traditions, or group participation. There is no right or wrong way to experience the day.

Our goal is simple: to provide a setting that feels respectful, unrushed, and supportive — allowing families to say goodbye in a way that feels natural and meaningful to them.

If you have questions or would like to discuss what a burial at sea might look like for your family, we encourage you to speak with a funeral director at any time.
