Frequently Asked Questions About Cremation in Chula Vista

Is a visit to the funeral home required?

Not at all. Most Chula Vista families complete arrangements by phone or online. You can arrange a face-to-face meeting, or even come to our offices, but it’s not necessary.

How soon can you pick up someone from Chula Vista?

Once we get notified, we have the necessary authorizations, and we then have to go through the appropriate channels for transportation. It can often depend on the situation and location of the passing. However, we coordinate with area hospitals, nursing homes, and hospice to eliminate delays whenever possible. All that said, we are generally there in about 1.5 hrs.

What is the time frame for cremation?

Typically, after securing the necessary legal documents, families can expect that the cremation will occur within a couple of days. We realize that families often find this process difficult, and to alleviate some of the worries, we simply keep you informed as things progress.

Is cremation more affordable than a burial?

In most cases, yes, burial services include additional costs that you will not have to worry about with a cremation; things like a casket, burial vault and even the service at the cemetery can cost more than the cremation options. Families are even able to schedule memorial services based on when relatives are able to come together, creating more time to plan.

Is it possible to have a memorial service at a later date?

Certainly. It is common for families to have a memorial or celebration of life service later after they have had the cremation done. It is helpful for families who live far apart. We can help arrange memorial services at funeral homes, churches, cemeteries, or other places they choose to.

Do you help with transportation to Mexico?

Yes. We regularly assist South Bay families with transportation and repatriation to Mexico, including Tijuana and other areas, on a regular basis. We take care of all Mexican consulates’ required documents, permits, translations, and border funeral home arrangements.

Do you have bilingual staff?

Yes. We take pride in serving the diverse community of Chula Vista, and we provide services in English and Spanish. Bilingual staff members assist Spanish-speaking families in all steps of the process.
