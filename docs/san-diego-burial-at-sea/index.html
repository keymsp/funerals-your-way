Burial at Sea in San Diego

Ash Scattering, Ocean Memorials & Sea Burial Services

Planning a burial at sea in San Diego can feel overwhelming, especially when families are unfamiliar with legal requirements, cremation arrangements, or what to expect during an at-sea ceremony. Questions about permits, timing, attendance, and coordination often arise during an already emotional time.

As a licensed San Diego funeral home with more than 20 years of experience, Funerals Your Way provides clear guidance through every step of the burial at sea process. We assist with cremation, required documentation, and planning details, then coordinate the ceremony with trusted local sea burial specialists to ensure everything is handled with dignity and full compliance with California regulations.

Why Families Choose Burial at Sea in San Diego

Burial at sea and ash scattering in San Diego have become increasingly popular as families learn about this peaceful and meaningful way to honor a loved one. For many, the ocean offers a sense of calm, openness, and connection that traditional settings may not provide.

A sea ceremony allows families to step away from formal environments and gather in a natural, healing space. Surrounded by open water, fresh air, and quiet reflection, many families find the experience deeply personal and emotionally grounding.

While many families choose cremation and ash scattering, others prefer a full body ocean burial. Learn more about full body burial at sea and how it differs from ash scattering.

San Diego’s year-round mild weather and protected coastal waters make it an ideal location for burial at sea ceremonies. Services can be planned comfortably throughout the year, with calm conditions that allow families to focus on remembrance rather than logistics.

Families sometimes travel from neighboring states to hold an ocean memorial here, including those arranging Arizona ash scattering at sea ceremonies when they wish to gather by the Pacific for a meaningful farewell. Environmental considerations are also important to many families. Sea burials and ash scatterings are conducted using biodegradable materials and ocean-safe floral arrangements.

For some, burial at sea reflects a lifelong connection to the ocean. For others, it represents simplicity, freedom, or spiritual symbolism. Whatever the reason, families often describe the experience as gentle, respectful, and profoundly meaningful.

How a Burial at Sea Works — Planning Through Ceremony

Arranging a burial at sea does not need to feel complicated or overwhelming. When handled by an experienced and licensed funeral home, the process is clear, organized, and thoughtfully coordinated from beginning to end.

From permits and documentation to scheduling and ceremony details, each step is carefully managed so families can focus on honoring their loved one, sharing meaningful moments, and finding peace — rather than worrying about logistics.

1. Planning & Necessary Permissions

The first step involves confirming cremation arrangements and completing the necessary paperwork, including the county disposition permit required for burial at sea. As a licensed San Diego funeral home, we handle these legal requirements on your behalf and explain each step clearly before a ceremony date is set.

Families who wish to plan ahead may also arrange these services in advance through pre-need planning, allowing wishes to be documented and easing the burden on loved ones later.

2. Welcome Aboard

On the day of the ceremony, families are welcomed aboard by the crew. The ceremony flow is reviewed, safety guidelines are explained, and everyone is given time to settle in comfortably before departure. This creates a calm, unhurried atmosphere from the beginning.

3. Traveling to the Scattering Location

Once underway, the vessel travels beyond the harbor to a calm, federally approved location at sea. In San Diego, the route often passes meaningful landmarks such as Point Loma and Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery, which many families find especially meaningful.

4. Words of Remembrance

When the vessel reaches the ceremony location, families are invited to share words, readings, prayers, or moments of silence. Some families choose to lead this portion themselves, while others prefer the crew to guide the ceremony gently and respectfully.

5. Ash Scattering & Flowers

Cremated remains are released into the ocean either directly or using a biodegradable container, depending on family preference. Flowers or petals are often placed into the water as a final tribute, allowing everyone present to participate in the farewell.

All materials used are environmentally safe, and the ceremony is conducted in compliance with all applicable regulations.

Biodegradable urns may be used for burial at sea when desired, and we can help families select an appropriate option during planning. Biodegradable urns for burial at sea

6. Return & Reflection

After the ceremony, families are given time for quiet reflection during the return to harbor. Many families describe this portion of the journey as peaceful and grounding — a moment to breathe, talk softly, or simply be together before returning home.

Burial at Sea Service Types

Families may choose from several types of ash scattering services, depending on whether they wish to attend the ceremony in person, observe from shore, or have the scattering performed on their behalf. Each option is handled with the same care, dignity, and full regulatory compliance, and we take time to explain the differences so families can choose what feels right for them.

Attended Ash Scattering at Sea

Having the family present aboard the vessel is the most common type of ash scattering at sea. Families are invited to participate in the ceremony by sharing words, placing flowers, or observing a quiet moment of reflection as cremated remains are released into the ocean.

This option often provides a strong sense of closure and allows loved ones to support one another in a peaceful, natural setting. Some families also choose to combine the ceremony with light sightseeing during the voyage.

Unattended Ash Scattering

When families are unable to attend the ceremony in person, we can respectfully perform the ash scattering at sea on their behalf. The same care, dignity, and regulatory compliance are followed as with attended services.

Families may request photographs, and a memorial certificate is provided after the scattering to confirm the date and location of the ceremony.

Documents Required for Unattended Ash Scattering

To proceed with an unattended ash scattering at sea, we will need the following:

  • A completed and signed authorization form
  • A copy of the authorizing person’s driver’s license or other state-issued identification
  • An original death certificate, which allows us to obtain the required county disposition permit
  • Payment, which may be arranged in advance or provided by check or credit card

If you have questions about any of these items, a licensed funeral director is available to guide you through the process at 619-550-7807.

View from Shore Ash Scattering at Sea

Shore viewing allows families to observe the ash scattering from land while the ceremony takes place offshore. This option is chosen when boat access is limited or when some family members prefer to remain on shore.

Common viewing locations include local piers and coastal areas such as Ocean Beach Pier, Crystal Pier, and select locations near Point Loma, depending on conditions and visibility.

Frequently Asked Questions About Ash Scattering at Sea

Is burial at sea legal in San Diego?

Yes. Burial at sea is legal when handled through a licensed funeral home and conducted at federally approved locations.

Do cremated remains need to be prepared in a specific way before burial at sea?

Yes. It is required to take the ashes out of the temporary cremation container and then either scatter the ashes into the ocean or use a water-soluble urn. In California, you may scatter ashes in the ocean, but it has to be 500 yards away from land, and you cannot leave any non-biodegradable items to float in the water. You must get a Disposition Permit before the ceremony. Funerals Your Way helps with the preparation and permits, while San Diego Burial at Sea coordinates the scattering service.

Can burial at sea include religious or military traditions?

Yes, burial at sea can be personalized, especially to incorporate any traditions the family may have, be they religious, cultural, or military. Incorporation of prayed/scriptured blessings or moments of reflection may be incorporated into the ceremony, and families may observe faith-based customs, so long as they comply with guidelines. For veterans, burial at sea may also include military recognition such as the flag, ceremony waiver, or service acknowledgement. Funerals Your Way helps the family to pre-plan these traditions, while San Diego Burial at Sea organizes the at sea ceremony to ensure these are done with dignity and respect.

What is the difference between attended and unattended ash scattering?

With an attended scattering of ashes ceremony, family and friends have the option of being present for the ceremony, usually taking place on a ship, where they have the opportunity to partake in the ceremony by way of readings, prayers, or flower placements into the ocean. This option provides the most opportunity for collective closure and remembrance. Unattended scattering consists of trained professionals conducting the scattering on the family’s behalf when unavailability is present. In all instances, the ceremony is performed tastefully and in accordance to the state of California.

Is there wildlife like dolphins or whales during the trip?

On our scattering trips it is common to spot many types of sealife. Sea lions are almost guaranteed and dolphins are common as well. The California Gray whale has also been spotted on many trips especially during the migration season (especially from December to April). Having the added benefit of seeing wildlife really makes the trip very special.

What’s the best time of day for calm waters?

This is really not a big issue with our charters as the water around here is generally quite calm. As well, most of the time is spent in the protected water of San Diego Bay. However, morning departures (especially before noon) typically offer the calmest seas. San Diego usually has excellent year-round weather, but wind picks up in the afternoon.

Can multiple family members attend, including children?

Yes. We have many vessels and can accommodate small, medium and large groups (over 100 people) and we provide safety equipment for all ages. The experience is designed to be peaceful, reflective, and family-friendly.
