Honoring Service

National Cemeteries of San Diego

Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery

Located at the tip of Point Loma overlooking the Pacific, Fort Rosecrans is one of the most significant military cemeteries on the West Coast, with roughly 100,000 service members and family interred. It is currently closed to new casket and cremated-remains interments. Each year, Wreaths Across America honors those laid to rest.

Miramar National Cemetery

Miramar National Cemetery sits about 15 miles north of downtown San Diego. Dedicated on January 30, 2010, this newer national cemetery serves veterans and their families throughout the region and is designed to accommodate roughly 250,000 veterans and eligible dependents.

Military Honors — Ash Scattering Ceremony

This ceremony took place before the family boarded the yacht for the ash scattering at sea. Military Honors are conducted prior to the veteran's interment, and at the conclusion the American flag is formally presented to the next of kin.

Military Honors — Miramar (U.S. Army)

Military Honors performed by the United States Army at Miramar National Cemetery. Following the ceremony, the American flag was respectfully presented to the next of kin.

Crossing the Bar — Coast Guard Auxiliary

Conducted by the U.S. Coast Guard Auxiliary prior to interment at Miramar National Cemetery. "Crossing the Bar" is a maritime tradition symbolizing a sailor's final voyage from this life into eternity, often including a reading of Alfred, Lord Tennyson's poem of the same name.

Ask the Funeral Director

Straight answers to common questions

Permanent Urns — Choosing the Right Urn

A large variety of permanent urns are available in materials such as wood, stainless steel, bronze, marble, and granite. They suit families who wish to keep cremated remains at home, place them in a columbarium niche, or inter them in a cemetery — a meaningful, enduring memorial.

Pre-Need Planning — Why Plan Ahead?

When arrangements aren't made in advance, the decisions and costs fall to family during a time of grief. Pre-planning lets you document your wishes, ease uncertainty, and provide financial clarity — a lasting gift of peace of mind to your loved ones.

Biodegradable Urns

There are many types of biodegradable urns made from natural materials like paper, sand, salt, and clay. They break down naturally in water or soil, making them ideal for burial at sea or green burial. Our team can help you choose the urn that fits your family's wishes.

Death Certificates — Top Questions Answered

Among the most common questions families ask are those about death certificates — how many certified copies they'll need, how long it takes, and how they're used for legal and financial matters. This video answers the five most frequently asked questions.

Who Can Sign Funeral Documents?

Families are often unsure who has the legal authority to authorize arrangements — a spouse, adult child, parent, or another designated individual. This short video clarifies who may sign funeral documents and how those decisions are determined under California law.

When Does the Funeral Home Get Involved?

When the funeral home becomes involved often depends on where and how the death occurred — at home, in a hospital, in a nursing facility, or under circumstances requiring the medical examiner. This video explains the scenarios and when the funeral home steps in to assist.

Coping With Grief

Grief affects people emotionally, physically, and spiritually, and there is no single "right" way to grieve. Lean on trusted friends and family, and consider professional counseling or grief support if feelings become overwhelming. You are not alone. Presented with Health Essential Hospice and Rev. Kathleen Glen.

Why a Service Matters

Many people initially say they don't want a service, but there are several meaningful reasons to consider one. A service is for the loved ones who remain — a chance to acknowledge the loss, share memories, and begin healing. Presented in partnership with Paramount Home Health and Hospice – Carla Stockton.
