Two Pearl Harbor Veterans Finally Laid to Rest at Fort Rosecrans

On December 7, 1941, thousands of American service members lost their lives in the attack on Pearl Harbor. For two of those fallen heroes, identification came decades later through modern forensic and DNA advancements, allowing them to finally receive the military burial they earned.

Our team was honored to assist in coordinating the interment of these two Pearl Harbor veterans at Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery in San Diego, ensuring full military honors were carried out with dignity, precision, and deep respect for their service and sacrifice.

Remembering December 7, 1941

On the morning of December 7, 1941, the United States naval base at Pearl Harbor was attacked by Japanese forces in a coordinated aerial assault. In less than two hours, 2,403 Americans were killed — including 2,335 service members and 68 civilians — and more than 1,100 were wounded. Eight battleships were damaged, along with numerous other vessels and nearly 200 aircraft.

The attack marked a turning point in American history, leading the United States to formally enter World War II the following day. President Franklin D. Roosevelt addressed Congress on December 8, calling the previous day “a date which will live in infamy.”

For many families, the loss endured far beyond that day. In some cases, service members were listed as missing in action, their remains unidentified for decades. Through advancements in forensic science and DNA analysis, some of those fallen heroes are now being identified and returned to their families — allowing them, at long last, to receive the military burial honors they earned.

Identification Through Modern Forensic Science

For decades, many service members lost at Pearl Harbor were listed as unknown or missing in action. Advances in forensic anthropology, dental comparison, and mitochondrial DNA testing have made it possible to identify remains that could not be confirmed in the 1940s.

Through the efforts of the U.S. military’s identification programs, families are sometimes notified generations later that their loved one has finally been accounted for. These discoveries often bring a complex mix of emotions — grief revisited, but also relief and closure.

In the case of these two Pearl Harbor veterans, scientific testing allowed the Navy to confirm their identities and return them to their families. After more than eighty years, arrangements could finally be made for a proper military burial.

Interment with Full Military Honors at Fort Rosecrans

Their services were held at Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery, a place of honor overlooking the Pacific Ocean. The interments included full military honors, reflecting the service and sacrifice each veteran made to this country.

Military honors traditionally include:

  • The folding and presentation of the United States flag
  • The playing of “Taps”
  • A ceremonial rifle volley
  • Formal acknowledgment of service by uniformed military personnel

Families who would like to better understand how military honors are arranged and what is included may visit our Veteran Military Honors page for detailed information.

These ceremonies require precise coordination with the appropriate military branch, cemetery officials, and the veteran’s family. Our team assisted in arranging each detail, ensuring the services were conducted with dignity, respect, and careful attention to protocol.

Honoring Veterans in San Diego

Military interments at Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery require close coordination with the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, the appropriate military branch, and cemetery administration. Eligibility verification, scheduling, honors coordination, and documentation must all be handled with precision.

For the families of these Pearl Harbor veterans, ensuring full military honors was an essential part of honoring their legacy. From the ceremonial rifle salute to the presentation of the American flag, each element of the service reflects the nation’s gratitude.

San Diego is home to one of the largest military communities in the country, and many families here are eligible for VA burial benefits and military honors. Families planning services may learn more on our Veterans Services page.

These benefits may include:

  • Interment in a national cemetery
  • Military honors ceremony
  • Government-issued headstone or marker
  • Burial flag
  • Presidential Memorial Certificate

Our role is to guide families through this process, assist with required paperwork, and coordinate directly with military representatives so that every detail is carried out properly and respectfully.

Paul Gebser was killed in action during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. Eventually, his bones were found by divers, and the Navy was able to perform a DNA test to confirm his identity.

Daniel Harris was killed in action at Pearl Harbor and was laid to rest at Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery on December 7, 2022.

A Long-Awaited Rest

For these two service members, more than eighty years passed before their names were formally spoken at their graveside. Through modern science and dedicated military recovery efforts, they were finally returned home.

Standing at the ocean’s edge at Fort Rosecrans, overlooking the Pacific, their interment served as a reminder that service and sacrifice are never forgotten.
