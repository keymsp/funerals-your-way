Five Best At Sea Scattering Places in San Diego

Why Families Choose San Diego for Ash Scattering at Sea

San Diego is one of the most peaceful and accessible locations on the West Coast for ash scattering at sea. Families often choose this coastline for its calm harbor conditions, open ocean views, and mild weather that allows ceremonies to take place comfortably throughout the year.

As a licensed San Diego funeral home (FD #2188), Funerals Your Way coordinates San Diego burial at sea services in partnership with trusted local providers, ensuring all planning and legal requirements are handled with care.

Most ash scattering journeys depart from protected waters near Point Loma and Shelter Island before passing beneath the Coronado Bridge and into the open Pacific. This gradual transition from harbor to ocean creates a quiet, reflective setting that many families find deeply meaningful.

San Diego is a prime location for scattering ashes at sea. From our experience doing it for many years, we believe these are the Five Best at Sea Ash Scattering Locations in San Diego. Ash scattering provides an opportunity to commemorate and remember your loved one with an important ceremony, often including speeches, toasts and prayers. It is a beautiful healing experience among friends and family on the ocean.

What to Expect During an Ash Scattering at Sea

Families often arrive unsure of how a sea scattering ceremony will unfold. While every service is personalized, most ash scatterings from San Diego follow a calm and thoughtful flow designed to allow time for reflection, remembrance, and closure.

Typically, guests are welcomed aboard the vessel, given a brief orientation, and then travel together toward the scattering location. Once at sea, families may share words, music, prayer, or silence before flowers and cremated remains are gently placed into the ocean. Some families also choose ceremonial elements such as biodegradable urns for sea burial, designed to dissolve naturally in ocean water. Afterward, there is time to reflect before returning to shore.

Learn more about what to expect during an ash scattering at sea, including arrival, ceremony flow, and the return to harbor.

We coordinate and conduct ash scatterings at sea throughout the year, with a calm, respectful ceremony tailored to each family.

Many families choose sea scattering for its simplicity, natural setting, and the comfort of being together on the water.

This simple expression offers a touching way to remember and celebrate that deep bond with the waves.

Cabrillo Monument (End of Point Loma)

When saying goodbye to someone special, an ash scattering at sea is a beautiful way to memorialize both their life and impact on you. The gentle waves will take their remains, letting them drift into the wide ocean where they can finally rest in peace.

Cabrillo National Monument can be found at the tip of Point Loma, near the spot where Juan Rodriguez Cabrillo first set foot on what is now California’s west coast. You can explore more of San Diego’s story at this park with sites like the historic Old Point Loma Lighthouse and nearby tide pools teeming with life. At its Visitor Center, there is an interactive display of sixteenth century navigational tools and armor, and films related to the Age of Exploration. This park is one of the premier spots to spot gray whales as they migrate annually through this region.

Out in front of the park you can see both up and down the coast for miles. At this location we are out in the ocean a ways to avoid the kelp beds that grow in this area. Although kelp grows in many locations, the kelp forests off the coast of San Diego are some of the richest and most developed beds on earth.

Point Loma, Hotel Del and Downtown Vista

This is probably our favorite spot for ash scattering at sea in the San Diego area. We go out to almost the end of Point Loma and turn left and move to the other side of Zuniga Jetty. The water here are calmer and in addition this area provides wonderful vistas. You can see Point Loma, downtown San Diego and the Hotel Del in the near distance and further afar Mexico and the Colorado islands. If family members have a tendency to get sea sick than this is the spot to go. We very rarely have an issue with someone not feeling well at this location.

Often there are various boats anchored nearby as this area is also a free anchorage.

Sunset Cliffs and Ocean Beach

A little further up the coast is Sunset Cliffs. It is a Natural Park and Beach in San Diego that offers stunning natural beauty as a popular gathering place, making it one of the finest locations to scatter ashes at sea. Boasting incredible ocean views from its rugged coastline and being home to diverse ecosystems. We may travel farther offshore to avoid kelp beds and find calmer open-water conditions, which can extend the charter time. This will definitely extend the normal 2 hour ash scattering charter by at least another hour.

At low tide, you have the chance to find fascinating tidal pools filled with lively anemones, curious crabs, and colorful starfish. Or why not go snorkeling through vibrant kelp beds near the coast? Surfers from all around the globe love visiting this beach; its challenging swells that even test veteran surfers!

People living nearby often head over to Sunset Cliffs because it’s beautiful and relaxing. It’s their go-to location for having a picnic or just kicking back. Additionally, this area is home to numerous restaurants and bars, such as Little Lion Cafe which serves breakfast and lunch favorites such as eggs Benedict, salads, and sandwiches, while the OB Noodle House features delicious menu items including noodles, sushi, and beers. Plus you can check out OB Historical Museum to learn about how geological processes shaped its cliffs as well as history of the Kumeyaay tribe who once resided here.

Coronado Island

Coronado Island lies across San Diego Bay from downtown. We move the yacht further south to get closer to the island, sometimes scattering in sight of an individual’s house. Coronado offers visitors its white sand beaches, sparkling waters, and friendly beach town atmosphere. A popular tourist destination, Coronado attracts sun seekers seeking sun, surf, sand, and the history of Hotel del Coronado.

Pacific Beach / La Jolla

Pacific Beach and the La Jolla area is another common location for ash scattering at sea. It is further up the coast still from Ocean Beach, so the yacht charter may be longer. We can arrange however to begin your charter from Mission Bay. This will significantly reduce your charter length as opposed to leaving from Point Loma. It’s a chance to create lasting memories with family and friends by scattering ashes near the area where the deceased lived – perhaps even in sight of their house.

Pacific Beach charms visitors with its lively community vibe and stunning oceanfront scenery. Picture yourself basking under the sun on pristine shores or engaging in numerous leisure pursuits. This location also offers modern conveniences that make it ideal for maintaining a well-rounded life. Pacific Beach also benefits from proximity to world-class educational institutions such as University of California San Diego.

Frequently Asked Questions About Ash Scattering at Sea

Families often have questions when considering an ash scattering at sea, especially when planning a ceremony in an unfamiliar setting. The following answers address some of the most common concerns and are intended to help you understand what to expect and feel confident in your choices.

Is ash scattering at sea legal in San Diego?

Yes. Ash scattering at sea is permitted when conducted in accordance with federal and state regulations. We handle all required guidelines and coordination on the family’s behalf to ensure everything is done properly and respectfully.

How far offshore does an ash scattering take place in California?

In California, ash scatterings at sea are permitted at least 500 yards from shore. All ceremonies are conducted in accordance with state and federal guidelines, and we ensure the scattering location is appropriate, respectful, and environmentally compliant.

Can family and friends attend the ceremony?

Yes. Families are welcome to attend and participate in the ceremony. Many choose to include readings, prayers, music, flowers, or personal remarks. Attendance limits depend on the vessel selected.

Can flowers or biodegradable urns be used?

Yes. Flower arrangements and biodegradable urns designed for sea scattering may be used. We help families select materials that dissolve naturally and comply with environmental guidelines.

What if family members cannot attend the ash scattering in person?

If family members are unable to attend, we can perform the ash scattering on their behalf. The ceremony is conducted with the same care, respect, and attention as if family were present, and we provide details afterward so families know when and where the scattering took place.

Choosing the Right Ash Scattering Location in San Diego

San Diego offers a rare combination of calm waters, open ocean access, and year-round weather that makes ash scattering at sea both practical and deeply meaningful. Whether families are drawn to the quiet waters near Point Loma, the historic passage beneath the Coronado Bridge, or the expansive horizon off the coast, each location provides a peaceful setting for remembrance and reflection.

For many families, an ash scattering at sea becomes more than a ceremony — it becomes a moment of connection, closure, and healing. Taking the time to choose the right location helps ensure the experience feels personal, respectful, and true to the life being honored.
