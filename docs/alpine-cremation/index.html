Frequently Asked Questions from Families in Alpine

Is a visit to the funeral home required for Alpine families?

No. Most Alpine families complete arrangements by phone or online, with in-person meetings optional.

How soon will you pick up someone from Alpine?

After we are notified, and have the needed authorizations, we schedule transport as soon as we can. Mostly we are there quickly in around 1.5 hrs. These are most dependent on location and circumstance, however, we have been working with most care facilities in order to avoid unnecessary delays.

How long does it take to get someone cremated?

Most cremations, from our experience, take only a matter of days to finish up, and that is only after us getting the required paperwork. We are very transparent during this process so that families are not kept in the dark. Most families have the ashes back with them in 1.5 weeks or so. For detailed information on our cremation services, have a look at our cremation services page.

Is it cheaper to get cremated instead of buried?

Typically yes. There is a lot of costs that go into burials like caskets, burial vaults, and cemeteries. These costs go away with cremation, making it the cheaper option. We take the time to go over the options so that the families can pick an option that fits their financial situation. If however a family is interested in burial we can provide full burial services.

Can we have a memorial service later?

Certainly. A memorial or celebration of life service can be conducted on a later date, which is what most families opt to do as they organize a cremation. When the time is right, we can assist you in coordinating these services.

What paperwork is required?

We prepare the necessary documents, such as permits and authorizations. Our staff helps families understand what is required, and keeps the paperwork under control whenever they can.

Do you work with local cemeteries and memorial parks?

Yes. We frequently help families with the burial or cremation services at the cemeteries serving Alpine and East County, and we assist with the coordination of other arrangements and scheduling as needed.

Is bilingual assistance available?

Yes. Families that prefer to communicate in Spanish can receive assistance from a Spanish-speaking staff member.
