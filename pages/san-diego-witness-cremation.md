## What Is Witness Cremation?

Witness cremation allows one or more family members to **be present at the cremation facility at the time cremation takes place**. Depending on family wishes, this may include a brief viewing, final prayers or words, and the opportunity to witness the placement of the deceased into the cremation chamber.

The process is conducted **privately and respectfully**, with staff present to guide families and explain what to expect. Families are never rushed, and participation is entirely optional — some choose to be present throughout, while others prefer to say goodbye before cremation begins.

Witness cremation is available only at facilities that allow family presence and **requires advance scheduling**, permits, and coordination. Our role is to handle these requirements so the experience is orderly, dignified, and compliant with all regulations.

## What to Expect on the Day of Cremation

On the day of witness cremation, **families are guided step by step through the process** by experienced staff. Before cremation begins, families may gather privately at the cremation facility for a brief viewing, final prayers, or quiet reflection, depending on their wishes.

A staff member will explain what will take place and answer any questions before proceeding. Families may choose how much they wish to participate — some prefer to remain present throughout, while others choose to say goodbye before cremation begins. There is no requirement to do more than what feels comfortable.

Every effort is made to ensure the experience is respectful, unhurried, and dignified. Once cremation is complete, the cremated remains are prepared for return to the family according to the arrangements selected.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Witness Cremation

### Is witness cremation required for certain religions?

No. While witness cremation is sometimes chosen for religious or cultural reasons, it is not required and is entirely optional. Families of many backgrounds choose it for personal or spiritual reasons.

### Do family members have to stay for the entire cremation?

No. Participation is flexible. Some families choose to remain present throughout, while others prefer to say goodbye before cremation begins. Families may decide what feels comfortable for them.

### Does witness cremation change the cremation process?

The cremation process itself is the same. Witness cremation simply allows families to be present at the cremation facility and observe the placement of their loved one into the cremation chamber.
