# Burial at Sea & Ash Scattering Services

Burial at sea is a meaningful way to honor a loved one in a setting that **feels open, natural, and deeply calming**. Whether through the scattering of cremated remains or a full-body burial at sea, families often find comfort in the simplicity, beauty, and quiet reflection the ocean provides.

At Funerals Your Way, we guide families through burial at sea with clarity, compassion, and **licensed funeral home oversight**. Our role is to ensure all planning, permits, and coordination are handled properly, while allowing your family to focus on remembrance and healing.

## Two Burial at Sea Options

Families typically choose one of the following services. Each option has its own considerations, which we explain clearly before any decisions are made.

If you have questions about regulations, distance offshore, or how burial at sea works in California, you can find clear answers in our [Burial at Sea FAQ section](/funeral-q-a/#sea).

### Ash Scattering at Sea (Cremation)

Ash scattering at sea is the most common burial-at-sea choice. It allows families to gather aboard a vessel, share words or quiet moments, and **gently release their loved one's cremated remains** into the ocean—often accompanied by flowers or biodegradable ceremonial elements.

Ash scattering services may be **attended by family**, **performed unattended on your behalf**, or **viewed from shore**, depending on preference.

Learn more about [ash scattering at sea in San Diego](/san-diego-burial-at-sea/)

### Full Body Burial at Sea

![full body burial at sea white roses](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/burial-at-sea-shroud-white-roses-cp.jpg)

A full body burial at sea is a highly regulated service that follows strict federal and environmental guidelines. This option is less common and requires specialized preparation, permitting, and coordination to ensure **full compliance with U.S. Coast Guard and Environmental Protection Agency** regulations. The vessel, burial container, and ceremony must all meet specific requirements, including distance from shore and ocean depth. Because of these complexities, full body burial at sea is typically arranged through a licensed funeral home working closely with experienced maritime professionals.

Learn more about [full body burial at sea requirements](/full-body-burial-at-sea/).
