## Shipping Full-Body Human Remains Internationally

Transporting a loved one internationally **requires coordination between multiple agencies** and strict compliance with destination country regulations. When shipping a casketed body overseas, we coordinate directly with the receiving funeral home, the appropriate consulate, and the airline's cargo division to ensure every requirement is met.

International shipments are typically transported as airline cargo through San Diego International Airport. We prepare the casket according to international shipping standards, including required sealing procedures and airline compliance regulations.

Processing time typically ranges from **one to three weeks, depending on the country's documentation requirements** and consular approval timelines.

### Documentation May Include

- Certified copy of the death certificate
- Transit permit issued in California
- Embalmer's affidavit
- Consular authorization paperwork
- Translation of documents (if required)
- Deceased's passport
- Receiving funeral home information

Because each country has unique regulations, we verify requirements before transport begins to prevent delays at customs or at the destination airport.

Our team manages all communication and logistics so families can focus on their loved one — not the paperwork.

Most international shipments take 1–3 weeks depending on documentation — **we'll guide you through each step**. We understand this process often takes place during a difficult time, and **our goal is to make everything as clear** and manageable as possible.

## Shipping Cremated Remains Internationally

Shipping cremated remains (cremains) internationally is generally less complex than transporting a full body, but it still requires proper documentation and coordination. Each country has specific entry requirements, and failure to provide the correct paperwork can result in delays at customs.

Cremated remains may be transported through airline cargo or international shipping services, depending on the destination country's regulations.

### Documentation Typically Required

- Certificate of cremation
- Certified copy of the death certificate
- Transit permit issued by the California funeral home
- Consular authorization (if required)
- Translated documents (when necessary)
- Name and address of the receiving party
- Deceased's passport (for certain countries)

Some countries require official consular stamps or prior authorization before cremated remains are permitted to enter. We verify all requirements in advance and coordinate with the appropriate authorities to ensure a smooth transfer. Certain countries require consular legalization before departure. In these cases, documents must be reviewed and stamped by the destination country's consulate prior to airline acceptance.

Our team manages the documentation, consular communication, and shipping logistics so families can proceed with confidence.

## Traveling Internationally with Cremated Remains

In some situations, families choose to **personally travel with an urn** rather than ship cremated remains separately. While this is often permitted, regulations vary by country and airline.

Before making travel arrangements, it is important to **confirm that the destination country allows individuals to transport cremated remains**. Some countries require shipment through a licensed funeral provider instead of personal transport.

![travelling internationally with cremains](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/travelling-internationally-with-cremains.webp)

**Important Travel Guidelines:**

- The urn must be X-rayable.
- TSA will not open an urn, even upon request.
- Carry-on luggage is strongly recommended to avoid loss or mishandling.
- Certain countries may require advance authorization or consular documentation.

**Documentation Typically Needed for Travel:**

- Certificate of cremation
- Certified death certificate
- Transit permit
- Declaration of contents from the funeral home
- Destination contact information

We strongly recommend verifying entry requirements before departure. Our team can confirm regulations with the appropriate consulate to help ensure a smooth and compliant journey.

## Frequently Asked Questions About International Shipping of Human Remains

### How long does international shipping of human remains take?

International transport typically takes between one and three weeks. The timeline depends on the destination country's documentation requirements, consular processing times, airline scheduling, and any required document translations. Some countries require additional authorization before departure, which may extend the timeframe.

### Do all countries require consular authorization?

Most countries require some form of documentation approval through their consulate or embassy before remains can enter the country. Requirements vary significantly. Some countries require formal permit stamps, while others require prior legalization of documents. We verify requirements before initiating transport to prevent delays.

### Can I personally travel with cremated remains internationally?

In many cases, yes. However, regulations vary by country. Some countries require cremated remains to be shipped through a licensed funeral provider rather than transported by an individual traveler. Airline policies may also differ. We recommend confirming both airline and destination country requirements before making travel arrangements.

### Does the urn need to meet special requirements for airline travel?

Yes. The urn must be X-rayable. TSA will not open an urn, even upon request. If the urn cannot be scanned clearly, it may not be permitted through security. Carry-on luggage is strongly recommended to avoid loss or mishandling.

### What documents are required to ship cremated remains internationally?

Documentation typically includes a certificate of cremation, certified death certificate, transit permit, and in some cases, consular authorization and translated documents. Certain countries also require a declaration of contents and receiving party information. Requirements vary by destination.

### Is embalming required for international shipment of a body?

Most countries require embalming for international shipment of full-body remains. An embalmer's affidavit is typically required as part of the documentation package. Additional preparation standards may apply depending on the destination country and airline regulations.
