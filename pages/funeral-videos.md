        <h3>Fort Rosecrans National Cemetery</h3>
        <p>Located at the tip of Point Loma overlooking the Pacific, Fort Rosecrans is one of the most significant military cemeteries on the West Coast, with roughly 100,000 service members and family interred. It is currently closed to new casket and cremated-remains interments. Each year, <a href="/wreaths-across-america-fort-rosecrans/">Wreaths Across America</a> honors those laid to rest.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="vid">
        <div class="video-embed"><iframe src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/7crKfBEOBGg" title="Miramar National Cemetery" loading="lazy" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
        <h3>Miramar National Cemetery</h3>
        <p><a href="/miramar-national-cemetery/">Miramar National Cemetery</a> sits about 15 miles north of downtown San Diego. Dedicated on January 30, 2010, this newer national cemetery serves veterans and their families throughout the region and is designed to accommodate roughly 250,000 veterans and eligible dependents.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="vid">
        <div class="video-embed"><iframe src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/0C7p8O86GhA" title="Military Honors — Ash Scattering Ceremony" loading="lazy" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>

        <p>This ceremony took place before the family boarded the yacht for the <a href="/san-diego-burial-at-sea/">ash scattering at sea</a>. <a href="/veteran-military-honors/">Military Honors</a> are conducted prior to the veteran's interment, and at the conclusion the American flag is formally presented to the next of kin.</p>

        <h3>Crossing the Bar — Coast Guard Auxiliary</h3>
        <p>Conducted by the U.S. Coast Guard Auxiliary prior to interment at <a href="/miramar-national-cemetery/">Miramar National Cemetery</a>. "Crossing the Bar" is a maritime tradition symbolizing a sailor's final voyage from this life into eternity, often including a reading of Alfred, Lord Tennyson's poem of the same name.</p>

        <p>When arrangements aren't made in advance, the decisions and costs fall to family during a time of grief. <a href="/pre-need-funeral-planning/">Pre-planning lets you document your wishes</a>, ease uncertainty, and provide financial clarity — a lasting gift of peace of mind to your loved ones.</p>

They break down naturally in water or soil, making them ideal for burial at sea or green burial. Our team can help you choose the urn that fits your family's wishes.</p>
