## What Is a Cremation Urn?

A cremation urn is a container designed to **hold the cremated remains of a loved one after cremation**. While its practical purpose is to safely contain the ashes, many families view an urn as a symbolic and personal memorial that represents remembrance, connection, and legacy.

Urns may be kept at home, placed in a columbarium or mausoleum, buried, or used temporarily before ashes are scattered. Because of these different uses, cremation urns are available in a wide range of sizes, materials, and designs—each suited to a specific purpose or setting.

Understanding how an urn will be used is often the first step in choosing the right option, helping families narrow their choices and select something that **feels appropriate and meaningful**.

## Types of Cremation Urns

Cremation urns are available in several types, each designed for a specific purpose or preference. Understanding these options can help families choose an urn that aligns with how they plan to memorialize their loved one.

**Standard urns** are designed to hold all of a person's cremated remains. These are commonly chosen when ashes will be kept at home, placed in a niche, or buried in a cemetery or mausoleum.

**Keepsake urns** are smaller containers that hold a portion of the cremated remains. Families often select keepsake urns when ashes will be shared among relatives or when most of the cremains will be scattered while a small amount is retained as a personal remembrance.

**Companion urns** are intended to hold the cremated remains of two individuals, such as spouses or partners. These urns are designed to symbolize connection and togetherness and are often chosen for long-term memorial placement.

## Cremation Urn Materials and Styles

Cremation urns are made from a wide variety of materials, each offering a different look, feel, and purpose. The right choice often depends on where the urn will be placed and what best reflects the personality or values of the person being remembered.

**Wood urns** offer a warm, traditional appearance and are often chosen for home display or placement in a columbarium. They range from simple designs to finely crafted pieces made from hardwoods such as oak, walnut, or cherry.

**Metal urns**—including bronze, brass, stainless steel, and aluminum—are durable and available in both classic and contemporary styles. These urns are commonly selected for burial, niche placement, or long-term storage.

**Stone and marble urns** provide a timeless, solid feel and are often chosen for permanent memorial locations. Their weight and durability make them well suited for mausoleums or cemeteries.

**Glass and ceramic urns** are frequently selected for their artistic qualities. Handcrafted designs and rich colors can make these urns feel like works of art, offering a unique way to honor a loved one's individuality.

**Biodegradable urns** are designed for environmentally conscious families. These urns naturally break down over time and are typically used for burial or water-based memorials, including ash scattering at sea.

Material and style choices are deeply personal. Selecting an urn that feels right can help families create a tribute that is both respectful and meaningful.

## Choosing the Right Size Cremation Urn

Selecting the correct urn size is an important step to ensure all cremated remains are properly accommodated. Most full-size adult urns are designed to hold approximately 200 cubic inches, which is suitable for the cremated remains of an average adult.

A helpful guideline many families use is **one cubic inch per pound of body weight before cremation**. For example, someone who weighed 180 pounds will typically require an urn with a capacity of at least 180 cubic inches. When in doubt, choosing a slightly larger urn provides peace of mind.

**Families may also consider alternative sizes depending on their plans:**

- Standard urns hold all cremated remains and are commonly used for burial, columbarium placement, or home display.
- Keepsake urns hold a small portion of the ashes and are often chosen when remains will be shared among family members or partially scattered.
- Companion urns are designed to hold the cremated remains of two individuals, often selected by spouses or partners.

## Where and How Cremation Urns Are Used

After selecting an urn, many families consider where the urn will ultimately be placed. The intended location often influences the type, material, and design chosen.

Some families choose to **keep the urn at home**, placing it in a quiet, meaningful space such as a bookshelf, mantel, or memorial area. When an urn is kept at home, families often prefer durable materials and designs that blend naturally with their décor.

Other families plan to place the urn in a **columbarium, mausoleum, or cemetery niche**. These locations may have size or material requirements, so it is helpful to confirm guidelines in advance. Many standard cremation urns are designed to meet common cemetery specifications.

Urns may also be used temporarily. In some cases, families keep the urn at home before scattering the ashes at sea, burying them, or transferring them to a permanent resting place. When scattering is planned, a portion of the cremains may be retained in a keepsake urn while the remainder is respectfully released.

Understanding how and where an urn will be used helps families choose an option that feels both practical and meaningful, while honoring their loved one's wishes.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cremation Urns

##### What is a cremation urn?

A cremation urn is a container designed to hold a person's cremated remains. Families may choose an urn for keeping at home, placing in a columbarium, burying in a cemetery, or using during a scattering ceremony. Urns are available in many materials and styles to reflect personal preferences and memorial plans.

##### Do I need to buy an urn after cremation?

Not necessarily. Many cremation providers return cremated remains in a temporary container. Families can keep that container, select a permanent urn later, or choose a specific urn based on whether the remains will be displayed, buried, or scattered.

##### What size urn do I need?

A general guideline is 1 cubic inch of urn capacity per pound of body weight. Most standard adult urns hold about 200 cubic inches, which is appropriate for many adults. Larger urns are available when needed, and smaller keepsake urns can hold a portion of cremated remains.

##### What materials are cremation urns made from?

Cremation urns are commonly made from metal, wood, ceramic, stone, glass, and biodegradable materials. The best material depends on how the urn will be used — whether displayed at home, placed in a niche, buried, or used for water or earth burial.

##### Can cremation urns be personalized?

Yes. Many urns can be engraved with a name, dates, or a meaningful message. Some allow for decorative artwork or other custom details. Personalization helps families create a tribute that feels unique and reflective of their loved one's life.

##### What is a biodegradable urn?

A biodegradable urn is designed to naturally break down over time. These urns are commonly used for burial in the earth or for water release ceremonies, including burial at sea. Families planning a scattering ceremony can learn more about [biodegradable urns for burial at sea](/biodegradable-urns-burial-sea/) and how they are designed to meet ocean-friendly guidelines. They provide an environmentally conscious option for those who prefer a natural return.

##### Can an urn be buried or placed in a cemetery?

Yes. Many urns are suitable for burial or placement in a cemetery columbarium. However, cemeteries may have specific size or material requirements, so it is helpful to confirm any guidelines before selecting an urn for that purpose.
