Burying a body at sea is a time-honored practice rooted in traditions that [stretch across cultures and religions for millennia](/full-body-sea-burial-history/). As an alternative to traditional land burial, this solemn ritual allows families to say a final farewell in the vast expanse of the ocean — a setting that reflects peace, openness, and a return to nature.

Modern full body sea burials are conducted with careful preparation and in accordance with federal maritime regulations to ensure dignity, safety, and environmental responsibility. In this guide, we explain the process, the ceremony, and what families can expect. For complete service details, visit our page on [Full Body Burial at Sea](/full-body-burial-at-sea/).

## The Ceremony for a Full Body Sea Burial

A burial at sea is often accompanied by a ceremonial tribute that honors the life of the person being remembered. The form of the ceremony can vary based on cultural traditions, religious beliefs, or the personal wishes of the individual and their family.

Families may include readings, prayers, music, or spoken reflections. Some prefer a quiet and intimate gathering, while others choose a more structured service led by clergy or a celebrant. The open horizon and movement of the ocean often create a peaceful setting that feels both solemn and expansive.

![Burying a Body at Sea - The Steps](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/burying-a-body-at-sea-steps-cp.png "")

For veterans or active service members, additional honors may be incorporated to recognize their service. This may include military readings, flag presentation, or coordination of ceremonial elements in accordance with service traditions. Families wishing to learn more about available options can review our page on [Veteran Burial Honors](/veteran-military-honors/).

Holding a service at sea allows family and friends to gather, reflect, and share memories in a meaningful environment. For many, the act of coming together on the water becomes an important part of the grieving and healing process.

## Preparation for a Full Body Burial at Sea

Before a burial at sea takes place, **careful preparations are made** to ensure the process is handled with dignity and in accordance with federal maritime regulations. Full body sea burials must meet specific environmental and depth requirements established by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and other governing authorities.

The body is respectfully placed in a specially prepared weighted shroud or casket designed to ensure a natural and permanent descent to the ocean floor. These materials must be biodegradable and free of substances that could harm the marine environment.

**Proper documentation and permits are obtained prior to departure**, and coordination with maritime guidelines ensures the burial occurs at the required distance from shore and at an appropriate depth. These safeguards help protect both the dignity of the ceremony and the integrity of the marine ecosystem.

When arranged through a licensed funeral home experienced in sea burials, families can feel confident that every step — from preparation to final committal — is conducted professionally and respectfully.

## Location and Vessel Selection

Full body burials at sea are conducted in designated offshore areas that **comply with federal maritime regulations**. In most cases, this means traveling several nautical miles from shore and reaching sufficient ocean depth to ensure a permanent and undisturbed resting place.

The specific location may be influenced by legal requirements, ocean depth, and the wishes of the family. Off the coast of San Diego, the Pacific Ocean provides deep-water locations that meet regulatory standards while offering a peaceful and expansive setting for the ceremony.

![Stable yachts are used](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/family-on-yacht-for-service.webp "")

The vessel selected for the burial plays an important role in the experience. **Stability, safety, and professionalism are essential**. Many families choose a vessel that allows close family members to gather comfortably while maintaining a solemn and respectful atmosphere.

Careful planning of both location and vessel helps ensure that the burial is conducted safely, lawfully, and with the dignity the moment deserves.

## The Natural Return to the Ocean

Once the ceremony concludes, the body is respectfully committed to the sea in accordance with maritime regulations. At the ocean's depths, **natural processes gradually return the body to the surrounding marine environment**.

Water temperature, depth, and environmental conditions influence the pace of this process. In deeper offshore waters — such as those found off the coast of San Diego — cooler temperatures and limited surface disturbance create a stable resting place.

Over time, **the body naturally integrates into the ocean ecosystem**. Many families find comfort in knowing that burial at sea reflects a return to nature — a peaceful transition into the vast and enduring rhythm of the sea.

![Natural Decomposition Process - Ocean Burial](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/natural-process-cp.png "")

Full-body **ocean burial promotes natural decomposition** with minimal environmental impact and the carbon is even sequestered in the ocean.

Burying a body at sea is a meaningful and deeply symbolic way to honor a life. For families who feel a connection to the ocean, it offers a dignified alternative to traditional land burial while respecting both personal wishes and environmental responsibility.

When conducted in accordance with federal maritime regulations and proper preparation standards, a full body sea burial **provides a peaceful and permanent resting place** in the vast expanse of the Pacific Ocean.
