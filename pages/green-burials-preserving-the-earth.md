## What Is a Green Burial?

A green burial — sometimes called a natural burial — is a method of disposition **designed to minimize environmental impact** and allow the body to return naturally to the earth. Unlike traditional burial practices that often involve embalming chemicals, metal caskets, or concrete vaults, green burial emphasizes simplicity and biodegradability.

In most green burials, the body is not embalmed and is placed in a biodegradable casket or natural fiber shroud. The burial typically takes place without a vault, **allowing natural decomposition to occur** in a way that supports the surrounding ecosystem.

![Green Burial Natural Scene](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/natural-scene-green-burial-cp.png "")

This approach reflects a growing interest in environmentally responsible practices while maintaining dignity and respect for the deceased.

## How Green Burial Differs from Traditional Burial

Traditional burial practices often involve embalming fluids, metal or hardwood caskets, and concrete burial vaults. While these methods are widely accepted and meaningful to many families, they can require significant natural resources and introduce non-biodegradable materials into the ground.

Green burial takes a different approach. It **typically avoids embalming chemicals, uses biodegradable caskets or shrouds**, and does not require a vault unless mandated by cemetery policy. The goal is to allow natural decomposition while minimizing environmental impact.

Cemetery landscaping may also differ. Traditional cemeteries often maintain manicured lawns and extensive irrigation systems, while natural burial grounds may preserve native vegetation and limit artificial landscaping.

By simplifying the burial process and reducing resource use, green burial offers an alternative that aligns with sustainability and environmental stewardship.

## Environmental Considerations of Green Burial

Green burial is designed to reduce the environmental impact commonly associated with conventional burial practices. By avoiding embalming chemicals and using biodegradable materials, this approach limits the introduction of synthetic substances into the soil.

Traditional burial methods may involve concrete vaults, metal caskets, and long-term landscape maintenance that require energy and natural resources. In contrast, green burial focuses on minimal intervention and natural decomposition.

Many natural burial grounds also emphasize land conservation. Some are established within protected landscapes or managed with native vegetation rather than manicured lawns. These practices can support biodiversity and reduce long-term resource consumption.

While **no disposition method is entirely without environmental impact**, green burial represents an option for families who wish to prioritize sustainability and a natural return to the earth.

## Green Burial Options in Southern California

Green burial availability varies by cemetery and region. In Southern California, some cemeteries offer designated **natural burial sections** where biodegradable caskets or shrouds are permitted and burial vaults may not be required. Policies can differ significantly between public and private cemeteries, so confirming specific requirements in advance is important.

**Families interested in green burial should ask about:**

- Whether embalming is required
- If burial vaults are mandatory
- What types of biodegradable containers are permitted
- Landscaping and maintenance practices
- Ongoing preservation policies

Some cemeteries emphasize land conservation and native landscaping, while others incorporate limited green burial sections within traditional memorial parks.

Because availability and regulations can change, speaking directly with cemetery staff — or working with a licensed funeral home familiar with local requirements — can help ensure that arrangements align with environmental preferences and legal guidelines.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Green Burial in San Diego

Families exploring environmentally conscious burial options often have practical questions. The answers below address common concerns about green burial practices, requirements, and availability in the San Diego area.

##### What is required for a green burial?

A green burial **typically avoids embalming** and uses biodegradable materials such as a natural wood casket or fabric shroud. Burial vaults are generally not used unless required by cemetery policy. Requirements may vary by cemetery, so confirming specific guidelines in advance is important.

##### Is embalming required for a green burial?

In most green burial practices, embalming is not required and is often avoided to prevent introducing chemicals into the soil. Refrigeration or other temporary preservation methods may be used if needed prior to burial.

##### Are burial vaults required for green burial?

Many natural burial grounds do not require vaults, as they can interfere with natural decomposition. However, some traditional cemeteries may still require vaults even within designated green sections. It is important to verify cemetery-specific policies.

##### How much does a green burial cost in San Diego?

The cost of a green burial can vary depending on the cemetery, burial container selection, and associated service fees. In some cases, costs may be comparable to traditional burial when vaults and embalming are excluded. Families should request detailed pricing from both the cemetery and funeral home to understand total expenses.

##### Are there dedicated green cemeteries in Southern California?

Some cemeteries in Southern California offer designated natural burial sections or environmentally conscious burial options. Availability can change, so contacting the cemetery directly or working with a licensed funeral home familiar with local practices is recommended.

##### Is green burial more environmentally friendly than cremation?

Green burial reduces the use of embalming chemicals and non-biodegradable materials. Cremation involves energy consumption and emissions. The environmental impact of each method depends on specific practices, and families should consider their personal values when making a decision.
