## What Is Alkaline Hydrolysis?

Alkaline hydrolysis, sometimes referred to as water cremation or aquamation, is a method of final disposition that uses water, heat, and alkaline compounds to accelerate the natural decomposition process. Unlike traditional flame-based cremation, which **relies on high heat and combustion**, alkaline hydrolysis uses a controlled water-based process within a sealed stainless-steel chamber.

![Alkaline hydrolysis image](https://funeralsyourway.com/wp-content/uploads/alkaline-hydrolysis-image.webp)

At the completion of the process, the remaining bone fragments are dried and processed into a form similar in appearance to traditional cremated remains. Families may choose to keep the remains in an urn, place them in a cemetery or columbarium, or scatter them in accordance with local and state regulations.

While the scientific process differs from flame cremation, the final options for memorialization are generally similar.

## How Alkaline Hydrolysis Differs from Traditional Cremation

The primary difference between alkaline hydrolysis and traditional cremation lies in the method used to reduce the body to remains. Traditional cremation uses high heat and flame, while alkaline hydrolysis uses a water-based process combined with heat and alkaline compounds within a sealed chamber.

From a family's perspective, however, the final result is similar. In both processes, the remaining bone fragments are returned to the family in a form suitable for placement in an urn, burial, or scattering according to local regulations.
