## National Cemetery Burial Benefits for Veterans

Eligible veterans may qualify for burial in a national cemetery, which can **include significant benefits** for both the veteran and their family. National cemeteries provide a dignified, well-maintained setting and relieve families of many long-term responsibilities associated with grave care. Eligibility and specific benefits depend on the veteran's service record and discharge status, and not all veterans qualify.

Burial benefits at a national cemetery may include a gravesite, opening and closing of the grave, a [government headstone or grave marker](/veteran-headstones-grave-markers-san-diego/), and perpetual care at no cost to the family. These benefits are administered by the Department of Veterans Affairs and require proper documentation and coordination at the time of need.

## Veteran Funeral Allowances & Cost Guidance

The Department of Veterans Affairs may provide funeral and burial allowances to help offset certain expenses when an eligible veteran passes. These benefits vary depending on whether the death was service-related or non–service-related, as well as the veteran's discharge status and place of burial. VA funeral allowances are typically provided as reimbursement and require proper documentation to be submitted after services are completed.

For **service-related deaths**, higher levels of assistance may be available. For **non–service-related deaths**, more limited reimbursement may apply toward burial and funeral expenses. Because eligibility requirements and benefit amounts can change, families are encouraged to confirm details directly with the VA or work with a funeral director experienced in coordinating veteran funeral benefits.

## Who Qualifies for Veteran Funeral Benefits

Eligibility for veteran funeral benefits is determined by the Department of Veterans Affairs and depends on a veteran's service history, discharge status, and other factors. While qualification rules can be complex, many families are surprised to learn that benefits may still be available even if eligibility is not immediately clear.

In general, **veteran funeral benefits may be available to:**

- Veterans who served on active duty and were discharged under conditions other than dishonorable
- Members of the Selected Reserve or National Guard who meet service requirements
- Service members who died while on active duty
- Certain spouses and dependents, depending on circumstances
